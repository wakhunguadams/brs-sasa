COMPANY REGISTRATION IN KENYA
-----------------------------

Types of Companies:
1. Private Company Limited by Shares (most common)
   - Minimum 1 shareholder, maximum 50
   - Name ends with "Limited" or "Ltd"
   - Cannot offer shares to the public
   
2. Public Company Limited by Shares
   - Minimum 2 shareholders, no maximum
   - Name ends with "Public Limited Company" or "PLC"
   - Can offer shares to the public
   
3. Company Limited by Guarantee
   - For non-profit organizations
   - Members guarantee to contribute on winding up
   
4. Unlimited Company
   - Members have unlimited liability

Requirements for Company Registration:
1. Proposed company name (must be unique and end with Ltd/Limited or PLC)
2. Physical address in Kenya
3. At least one director (must be a natural person, 18+ years)
4. At least one shareholder
5. Company secretary (for public companies, must be qualified)
6. Memorandum and Articles of Association
7. Copies of ID/Passport for all directors and shareholders
8. KRA PIN certificates for all directors
9. Passport photos for all directors

Company Registration Process via e-Citizen:
Step 1: Create an e-Citizen account at https://accounts.ecitizen.go.ke
Step 2: Log in and select "Business Registration Service"
Step 3: Apply for Name Search and Reservation
Step 4: Once name is approved, proceed with company registration
Step 5: Fill in company details (directors, shareholders, share capital)
Step 6: Upload required documents
Step 7: Pay registration fees via M-Pesa, card, or bank transfer
Step 8: Receive Certificate of Incorporation (usually within 24-48 hours)

Company Registration Fees (Approximate - verify current rates):
- Name Reservation: KES 150
- Private Company Registration: KES 10,650 (nominal capital up to KES 100,000)
- Additional stamp duty based on share capital
- Certificate of Incorporation: Included
- Certified copies: KES 500 per copy

BUSINESS NAME REGISTRATION
--------------------------

A Business Name (Sole Proprietorship) is suitable for:
- Sole proprietors (Sole Proprietorship)
- Partnerships (not LLPs)
- Simple business structures

Requirements:
1. Proposed business name
2. Nature of business
3. Principal place of business (physical address)
4. Full names and addresses of all proprietors/partners
5. Copy of National ID or Passport
6. KRA PIN certificate

Business Name Registration Process:
Step 1: Search for name availability on e-Citizen
Step 2: Reserve the business name
Step 3: Complete registration form
Step 4: Pay registration fees
Step 5: Receive Business Name Certificate

Business Name Registration Fees:
- Name Search: KES 150
- Registration: KES 950
- Certificate: Included
- Annual renewal not required (one-time registration)

LIMITED LIABILITY PARTNERSHIP (LLP)
-----------------------------------

An LLP combines features of a partnership and a company:
- Partners have limited liability
- Flexibility of a partnership
- Separate legal entity

Requirements:
1. Minimum 2 partners
2. At least one designated partner must be a Kenyan resident
3. Registered office address in Kenya
4. LLP Agreement
5. Compliance with beneficial ownership regulations

LLP Registration Fees:
- Name Reservation: KES 150
- LLP Registration: KES 10,650
- LLP Agreement filing: KES 2,000

FOREIGN COMPANY REGISTRATION
----------------------------

Foreign companies can operate in Kenya by:
1. Registering a branch office
2. Incorporating a subsidiary (local company)

Requirements for Foreign Company Registration:
1. Certified copy of Certificate of Incorporation from home country
2. Certified copy of charter/statutes/memorandum
3. List of directors
4. Local representative appointment (must be Kenyan resident)
5. Registered office address in Kenya
6. Power of Attorney

Foreign Company Registration Fees:
- Registration: KES 25,000+
- Local representative filing: KES 500

BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------

All companies and LLPs must maintain a register of beneficial owners:
- A beneficial owner is a natural person who ultimately owns or controls the entity
- Threshold: 10% or more shareholding or voting rights
- Must be reported to BRS within 14 days of any change

Required Information:
- Full name and date of birth
- Nationality and residential address
- ID/Passport number
- Nature and extent of beneficial interest
- Date of becoming beneficial owner

NAME RESERVATION GUIDELINES
---------------------------

Prohibited Names:
- Names identical or similar to existing registered names
- Names containing offensive or obscene words
- Names suggesting government/royal patronage without approval
- Names containing "Bank", "Insurance", "University" without relevant license
- Names misleading as to the nature of business

Name Reservation:
- Valid for 30 days
- Can be renewed once for additional 30 days
- Reserved name must be used for registration within validity period

ANNUAL COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------

Companies must file:
1. Annual Returns - within 42 days of anniversary
2. Financial Statements (for certain companies)
3. Change of directors/secretary within 14 days
4. Change of registered office within 14 days
5. Change of share capital within 14 days

Penalties for Late Filing:
- Annual returns: KES 5,000 + KES 500 per month of delay
- Other forms: Varies by document type

COMMON ISSUES AND TROUBLESHOOTING
---------------------------------

Name Rejection Reasons:
1. Name too similar to existing registered name
2. Contains restricted words without approval
3. Misleading or offensive content
4. Does not end with appropriate suffix (Ltd, PLC, LLP)

Payment Issues:
- M-Pesa: Ensure phone number is registered correctly
- If payment deducted but not reflected, wait 24 hours
- Contact BRS with payment reference for assistance

Document Rejection Reasons:
1. Poor quality scans
2. Missing required information
3. Inconsistent information across documents
4. Expired identification documents

E-COLLATERAL REGISTRY (MPSR)
----------------------------

The Movable Property Security Rights Registry allows:
- Registration of security interests in movable property
- Search for existing encumbrances
- Priority determination for creditors

Types of Collateral:
- Vehicles and equipment
- Inventory and accounts receivable
- Intellectual property
- Agricultural products

HIRE PURCHASE REGISTRY
----------------------

Registration of hire purchase agreements:
- Protects rights of owners

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
--------------------------

Q: Can a foreigner own 100% of a Kenyan company?
A: Yes, in most sectors. Some sectors like telecommunications and insurance have local ownership requirements.

Q: Do I need a Kenyan director?
A: Not mandatory for private companies, but recommended. At least one director must be a natural person.

Q: How long does company registration take?
A: Typically 24-48 hours through e-Citizen if all documents are in order.

Q: What is the minimum share capital?
A: There is no minimum share capital requirement for private companies. However, the nominal capital affects registration fees.

Q: Can I register a company from outside Kenya?
A: Yes, through e-Citizen platform or through a local agent with Power of Attorney.

Q: What is the difference between a company and a business name?
A: A company is a separate legal entity with limited liability. A business name is not a separate legal entity - the owner(s) are personally liable.

Q: How do I change company directors?
A: File Form CR12 (Notification of Change in Directors) within 14 days of the change.

Q: Can I transfer shares in my company?
A: Yes, by completing a share transfer form and updating the register of members.

Q: What happens if I don't file annual returns?
A: Penalties accrue, and the company may be struck off the register if non-compliant for extended periods.

Q: How do I close/dissolve a company?
A: Through voluntary winding up (solvent) or compulsory winding up (insolvent), following the procedures in the Companies Act and Insolvency Act.
